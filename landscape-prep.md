# Retaining Wall — Project Documentation

## Project Overview

This project involves the construction of a concrete and rock retaining wall to level a backyard with fill dirt. The wall runs along a straight 63-foot section flanked by two angled bank sections, one on each end, each 10 feet long and set at 120 degrees relative to the straight run. The total wall length including both banks is 83 feet.

The wall stands 5 feet tall. The face of the wall (the side against the fill dirt and raised backyard) will be poured concrete using Quikrete 60 lb bags. The back of the wall (the exposed, lower side) will be faced with a 3-inch rock layer. Total wall thickness is 10 to 12 inches. Wooden posts are already installed and set in concrete. Rocks have been acquired and are on-site.

---

## Wall Geometry

**Straight section:** 63 feet long, 5 feet tall, 10 to 12 inches thick.

**Banks:** Two angled sections, one at each end of the straight wall. Each bank is 10 feet long, 5 feet tall at the end joining the straight wall, and 2 feet tall at the far end. Both banks are set at 120 degrees from the straight wall.

**Total run:** 83 feet (63-foot straight + 10-foot bank at each end).

---

## Wall Construction

### Wall Layers (face to back)

The wall cross-section from the fill/face side to the exposed/back side consists of the following layers:

- **Concrete layer (face side):** 7 to 9 inches thick, Quikrete 60 lb bags, poured against the fill dirt side. This is the structural face of the wall.
- **Wood post:** 6-inch × 4-inch × 96-inch posts, installed at 7-foot on-center spacing along the straight section and banks. The 6-inch dimension is oriented toward the fill/face side. Posts are buried 3 feet below the lower natural grade and stand 5 feet above it.
- **Rock layer (back side):** 3 inches thick, placed on the exposed/back side of the wall. Rocks are already acquired and on-site.

### Posts

Posts are 6-inch × 4-inch × 96-inch dimensional lumber, installed at 7-foot on-center spacing. Each post is buried 3 feet below the lower natural grade with the remaining 5 feet above grade. The 6-inch face is oriented toward the fill side. Posts are already installed.

### Wall Footing

A concrete footing runs the full 83-foot length of the wall (straight section plus both banks). The footing is 18 inches wide and 6 inches thick. Rebar is embedded in the footing for reinforcement.

---

## Deadman Anchors

Deadman anchors are concrete elements embedded in the fill dirt on the face side of the wall to resist lateral soil pressure. They are installed along the straight section only — the banks do not receive deadmen.

**Count:** 7 deadmen total.

**Spacing:** Starting 3 feet from the left post, then every 10 feet. Positions along the straight wall (measured from the left end post): 3, 13, 23, 33, 43, 53, and 63 feet.

Two deadman configurations are under consideration:

### Configuration A — Horizontal Deadman

A horizontal concrete beam, approximately 4 feet long and 8 inches × 8 inches in cross-section, extends from the wall face into the fill dirt. At the far end, a vertical concrete footer block (18 inches × 18 inches × 12 inches) anchors the assembly. Two rebar bars run the length of the beam. Wire rebar ties connect the beam rebar to the wall's vertical rebar at the wall face.

### Configuration B — Diagonal Deadman (per original hand sketch)

A diagonal concrete strut attaches to the wall face at 4 feet above the base of the wall (1 foot below the fill surface) and runs at approximately 32 degrees down into the fill, reaching a horizontal concrete footing (24 inches × 8 inches × 8 inches) at the lower grade level. The diagonal strut is approximately 7.5 feet long and 8 inches × 8 inches in cross-section. Two rebar bars run the length of the strut, and wire rebar ties connect to the wall rebar at the attachment point.

---

## Drainage System

A perforated 4-inch PVC drain pipe, 64 feet long, runs along the base of the wall on the fill/face side. The pipe is positioned just above the wall footing, seated on a gravel bed, and covered by landscape fabric to prevent soil infiltration. The holes face upward to collect groundwater percolating down through the fill.

At each end of the straight wall, the pipe turns through the bank section and exits on the back (lower/exposed) side of the wall. Each exit connects to a corrugated flexible PVC drain tube to route collected water away from the wall. The length of the corrugated exit tubes will be determined by final site grading.

**Drainage assembly from bottom to top:** gravel bed → perforated PVC pipe (holes up) → landscape fabric → fill dirt.

---

## Rebar

Rebar is used throughout the wall, footing, and deadman assemblies for structural reinforcement. Wire ties are used to connect rebar at intersections.

**Wall rebar breakdown:**

- Horizontal bars: 240
- Vertical bars: 14
- Side bars: 10
- **Wall subtotal: 264 pieces**

**Deadman rebar (per configuration, × 7 deadmen):**

- Configuration A: 2 bars per beam + 4 bars per footer block = 42 pieces
- Configuration B: 2 bars per strut + 4 bars per footing = 42 pieces

**Total rebar: 306 pieces** (264 wall + 42 deadman, same for both configurations).

Rebar ties: 1,000-pack (sufficient for both configurations).

## Construction Notes

- Pour concrete in sections to allow curing between pours. Do not attempt to pour the full wall length in a single session.
- Deadmen should be poured and cured before backfilling with dirt begins in that section.
- The perforated drain pipe must be installed and the gravel bed placed before any fill dirt is added.
- Landscape fabric should lap over both sides of the pipe and extend at least 18 inches in each direction to prevent soil migration into the gravel and pipe.
- Both ends of the PVC drain pipe exit through the bank sections on the back (lower/exposed) side of the wall. The corrugated connection tubes must daylight at a lower elevation than the pipe to ensure gravity drainage.
- Rebar ties at deadman-to-wall connections should be snug to prevent movement during concrete pours.
- The banks do not receive deadman anchors. Lateral stability for the bank sections is provided by the post embedment depth and footing.
